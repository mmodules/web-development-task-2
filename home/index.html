3x3 Cube

The 3x3 is the most popular cube in the world. It's a great starting point for beginners and a challenge for experienced cubers.

This puzzle is a classic example of a twisty puzzle, where the goal is to align all the colors on each face of the cube.

Since it is the original twisty puzzle, it has inspired countless variations and innovations in the speedcubing community. It remains a cornerstone of the sport.

2x2 Cube

The 2x2 is a smaller version of the 3x3 cube. It's a much shorter event, and times at the top level are typically below 2 seconds.

Although it is a simpler puzzle, it still requires advanced solving techniques and algorithms to achieve fast times.

The key to solving it quickly lies in planning as far ahead as possible during your inspection phase.

4x4 Cube

The 4x4 is a larger version of the 3x3 cube. It's quite a bit longer, and times at the top level are usually around the 30 second mark.

The 4x4 introduces parity issues that don't occur on the 3x3, making it a more complex puzzle to solve.

Parity errors are also a luck based aspect of the puzzle.

Pyraminx

The Pyraminx is a tetrahedron-shaped twisty puzzle. It's quite a short event, and the best speedcubers can solve it in under 2 seconds.

Despite its unique appearance, it's a very easy puzzle to learn and solve.
